## Summary

At first glance, the business appeared healthy: revenue, profit, and sales volume were all growing.

However, a deeper analysis revealed a more nuanced story.

While revenue continued to increase, profitability was deteriorating across some of the company's largest customer segments. Certain categories generated significant sales but contributed very little profit, while several high-margin products remained underutilized despite their strong performance.

To help stakeholders identify these patterns quickly, I designed a fully interactive executive dashboard that enables users to analyze performance across customers, products, categories, and time through a single interface.

The result is a decision-support tool that transforms raw data into actionable business insights.

## Business Problem

Leadership teams often rely on high-level KPIs such as revenue and units sold to evaluate business performance.

While useful, these metrics alone can hide critical issues:

* Revenue growth without corresponding profit growth
* Declining margins within key customer segments
* Product categories consuming resources but generating little return
* Missed opportunities among high-performing products
* Seasonal patterns that impact planning and resource allocation

The goal of this project was to build a dashboard capable of answering a simple but important question:

> **Where should the business focus its efforts to maximize profitable growth?**

## Solution

I developed an executive-level Tableau dashboard that consolidates four years of sales performance into a single analytical experience.

The dashboard enables stakeholders to:

* Monitor key business KPIs
* Compare revenue, profit, profit ratio, and quantity sold
* Evaluate customer segment performance
* Analyze category and subcategory profitability
* Identify growth trends and margin risks
* Explore seasonal purchasing patterns
* Generate actionable recommendations based on data

A dynamic metric selector allows every visualization to instantly switch between Sales, Profit, Profit Ratio, and Quantity, enabling multiple analytical perspectives without leaving the dashboard.

## Dashboard Design Approach

The dashboard was designed with a single objective: **help decision-makers identify opportunities and risks.**

Rather than creating a collection of charts, I structured the dashboard around how executives naturally consume information.

### - Information Hierarchy

The layout follows a top-down analytical flow:

1. **KPIs** provide an immediate snapshot of business performance.
2. **Trend analysis** explains how performance evolved over time.
3. **Category-level views** reveal where growth and profitability are generated.
4. **Subcategory details** allow deeper investigation into specific products and operational drivers.

This layered approach enables users to move from high-level performance monitoring to detailed analysis without losing context.

### - Interactive Experience

To reduce dashboard complexity and improve usability, I implemented a dynamic metric selector that allows users to switch between:

> Sales, Profit, Profit Ratio, & Quantity

This transforms the entire dashboard instantly, providing multiple analytical perspectives without requiring separate reports.

### - Design Principles

The dashboard was built around several core principles:

* **Clarity over complexity** - every visual serves a specific business question.
* **Progressive disclosure** - users can move from summary metrics to detailed insights naturally.
* **Accessibility** - color choices and visual indicators were selected to remain readable for all users.
* **Actionability** - conditional formatting highlights important opportunities and risks automatically.

### - Outcome

The result is an executive dashboard that balances simplicity and analytical depth, enabling stakeholders to quickly understand performance, identify emerging issues, and make data-driven decisions with confidence.

## Key Insights

<br>

### --- Home Office Is the Fastest-Growing Segment

Home Office delivered the strongest year-over-year growth while consistently maintaining the highest profitability levels across the business.

Despite being the smallest segment, its growth rate and efficiency suggest significant untapped potential.

<br>

### --- Corporate Revenue Increased While Profit Declined

One of the most important findings was the disconnect between revenue and profit within the Corporate segment.

While sales increased, profit declined, indicating potential issues related to discounting, pricing strategy, or product mix.

<br>

### --- Furniture Generates Revenue but Very Little Profit

Furniture produced substantial revenue but operated at a significantly lower profit margin than Technology and Office Supplies.

This suggests pricing, discounting, or cost-related challenges that require further investigation.

<br>

### --- Hidden Profit Drivers Exist Across the Product Portfolio

Products such as Paper, Labels, Envelopes, and Copiers consistently generated some of the highest profit margins in the dataset.

These products represent opportunities for targeted promotions, bundling strategies, and recurring purchase programs.

<br>

### --- Revenue Growth Is Outpacing Profit Growth

Although the business expanded throughout the four-year period, profitability did not improve at the same pace.

This signals potential margin compression and highlights the importance of balancing growth with efficiency.

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, several strategic actions emerged:

### High Priority

* Investigate the decline in Corporate profitability despite growing sales.
* Review pricing and discounting strategies within the Furniture category.
* Analyze changes in product mix and average selling price across major segments.

### Growth Opportunities

* Increase investment in the Home Office segment.
* Expand promotion of high-margin product subcategories such as Copiers.
* Develop repeat-purchase initiatives for Paper, Labels, and Envelopes.

### Long-Term Monitoring

* Track average selling price as a core KPI.
* Monitor margin trends alongside revenue growth.
* Establish profitability targets at the category level.
